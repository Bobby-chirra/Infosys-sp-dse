How to Approach Coding During Exam/Interview

Clarify & Plan

  • Clarify problem input/output and constraints (ask 1–2 clarifying Qs)
  • Give a 30-second plan (approach and complexity)

Execution Strategy

  • Start with a simple correct solution (even brute force) then optimize
  • Talk while coding — narrate edge cases and thought process

Testing & Recovery

  • Write test cases and run through them manually
  • If stuck, explain tradeoffs and what you'd try next

Last-Minute Exam Tactics

Time Management

  • Read full paper first (if multiple sections)
  • Solve easy questions first
  • Don't waste >30 mins on a single coding question

MCQ Strategy

  • Eliminate wrong options first
  • Mark flagged ones to revisit

Notes & Fallback

  • Keep one page of hand-written notes (formulas, snippets)
  • If stuck: move on and come back later

What I Would Do If I Were You Right Now

Step 1: Language Choice

Pick your primary language (Java recommended for Infosys DSE). Stick with it for all practice.

Step 2: Start Schedule

Start Day 1 schedule immediately. Use 90–120 minute focused sprints, 15 min breaks.

Step 3: Track Progress

Keep a simple doc of mistakes & one cheat-sheet. Do one mock timed test tonight.
